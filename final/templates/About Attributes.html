Total Bilirubin:

Bilirubin is a yellowish pigment produced during the breakdown of red blood cells. Total bilirubin represents the overall level of bilirubin in the blood, which includes both direct (conjugated) and indirect (unconjugated) bilirubin. Elevated levels may indicate liver or gallbladder dysfunction.

Blood Report: Liver Function Test (LFT)

Direct Bilirubin:

Direct bilirubin refers to the fraction of bilirubin that has been processed by the liver and is excreted into the bile. Increased levels may indicate liver or bile duct issues.

Blood Report: Liver Function Test (LFT)

Alkphos Alkaline Phosphatase:

Alkaline phosphatase is an enzyme found in various tissues, including the liver, bones, and intestines. Elevated levels of alkaline phosphatase in the blood may indicate liver or bone disorders.

Blood Report: Liver Function Test (LFT)

Sgpt Alamine Aminotransferase:

Alamine aminotransferase, also known as alanine aminotransferase (ALT), is an enzyme found predominantly in the liver. Increased levels of ALT in the blood may suggest liver damage or injury.

Blood Report: Liver Function Test (LFT)

Sgot Aspartate Aminotransferase:

Aspartate aminotransferase (AST) is an enzyme found in the liver, heart, muscles, and other tissues. Elevated levels of AST in the blood may indicate liver damage or injury, but it is less specific to the liver compared to ALT.

Blood Report: Liver Function Test (LFT)

Total Proteins:

Total proteins refer to the total amount of proteins in the blood, including albumin and globulins. They are essential for various body functions and play a role in maintaining fluid balance, transporting substances, and supporting immune function.

Blood Report: Total Protein Test

ALB Albumin:

Albumin is a protein produced by the liver and is responsible for maintaining the oncotic pressure in the blood. Low levels of albumin can indicate liver dysfunction or malnutrition.

Blood Report: Albumin Test

A/G Ratio Albumin and Globulin Ratio:

The albumin-to-globulin ratio (A/G ratio) is the ratio of albumin to the total amount of globulins in the blood. It provides information about the balance between these two protein groups and can be indicative of certain diseases or conditions.

Blood Report: Serum Protein Electrophoresis
